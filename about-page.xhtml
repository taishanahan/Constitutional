Background

The former soviet rebuilt countries emerged as independent soveirgn states after the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991. With 15 states, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Estonia, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Latvia, Lithuania, Moldova, Russia, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, and Uzbekistan preceded their socialists republics and are internationally considered the successors of their corresponding Union Republics and not the Soviet Union. The three Baltic states, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, prioritized integrating into the international community, which clashed with the near-total disengagement with the Russian-dominated post-Soviet sphere, and eventually joined the European Union (EU) and the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO). The forms of governments of the new states varies from dominant-party to parlimentan to unitary presidential systems, however, Russian President Vladimir Putin has still dictated the Eastern region (Belarus, Moldova, and Ukraine) to be under Russia’s “sphere of influencer”. Economic recessions that followed the USSR’s dissolution marked a period of the fall of the Gosplan, the state planning agency responsible for economic planning, and the rapid embracement of market economic policies. This resulted in “shock therapy” policies in the early 1990s, where trade liberalization, price liberalization, and privatization triggered a drop in GDP and budget cuts towards social programs like education and healthcare. Therefore, some common trends found in the original constitutions drafted for each state was molded by this acceptance of a liberal economy, with instituions like the central bank and private property explicitly mentioned as independent to the state institions. Pairing this with social policies, privatization and liberalization formed the foundations of citizens’ liberties within each constitution.

Methods

Our team, to ensure an efficacious work process and acessible reading process, worked to define the elements that comprised the analysis of these documents. These elements are not all-encompassing, but are instead designed to be applicable to the body of text so as to make data collection informative and robust, but also efficient.

  • ind_rights

    Any guarantee of individual rights.

    ind_type

    • protest
    • citizenship
    • assembly
    • legal
    • vote
    • racial-equality
    • liberty
    • misc

  • nationalism

    Any mention of national pride, history, patriotism, or responsibility

  • religion

    Any mention of religion, it's freedoms, or its role with relation to the state.

    r_type

    • secular
    • state-religion
    • neutral

  • gov

    Miscellaneous reference to government role and design.

  • sovereignty

    Reference to the right of the individual state's right to exist.

  • capital

    Explicit designation of a capital city.

  • currency

    Explicit designation of a currency.

  • consent

    The consent of the governed. Anything that references that government or power lies with the people of the state.

  • elections

    Reference to electoral proceedings. Not suffrage, but rather what elections will be held and how.

  • separation

    Separation of powers, different branches of government (executive, legislative, judicial).

  • parties

    Reference to political parties and their definition, permittance, or prohibition.

    p_type

    • 0
    • 1
    • 2
    • multi

  • property

    Reference to rights of public or private ownership.

    prop_type

    • public
    • private
    • other

  • language

    Explicit mention of a specific language.

    lang_type

    • native
    • russian

  • international

    Any reference to international politics or association. The role the state intends to have internationally.

  • symbols

    Official symbols representing the state including flags, anthems, coats of arms, etc.

  • peace

    Reference to harmony or order.

  • treason

    Reference to attempts to usurp the powers that be in the state. Any attempt to illegitimately remove established government.

  • levels

    Mention of levels of government (national, state, local, etc), their powers, and how they interact with one another.

    l_type

    • unitary
    • federalist
    • neutral

  • economic

    Explicit definition of a state economic system

    e_type

    • free
    • state
    • mixed
